Project Information
Introduction

Over the years, the use of airports in Sri Lanka has surged dramatically, driven by an increase in international travel and a growing tourism industry. This rapid expansion has brought with it challenges such as long queues, delays, and operational inefficiencies. Hawk is designed to streamline and enhance the overall departure experience by automating key aspects of the departure process. Hawk manages to significantly reduces wait times, minimizes human error, and ensures a smoother flow of passengers, thus improving overall airport efficiency and passenger satisfaction.While automation brings immense benefits, it also introduces new threats. Automated systems can be targets for cyber-attacks, identity fraud, and unauthorized access. To mitigate these risks, Hawk incorporates advanced security features, including biometric authentication of individuals, access control and authentication, fraud detection and prevention, user behavior analysis, and secure data storage and transfer using encryption. These measures ensure that the departure process remains not only efficient but also secure against emerging threats. In a world where automation is the future and security is paramount, Hawk is the comprehensive solution to revolutionize airport departures in Sri Lanka. By seamlessly integrating efficiency with robust security measures, Hawk ensures a faster, safer, and more reliable departure process for all passengers. Embrace the future of airport automation with Hawk—where efficiency meets security.

Key Features

Hawk incorporates several advanced features to ensure a secure and efficient airport departure process. For biometric authentication of individuals, Hawk utilizes facial landmark detection technology, providing quick and accurate identity verification. Access control and authentication are enhanced through Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) protocols, ensuring that only authorized personnel can access restricted areas and systems without exposing sensitive information. To combat fraud, Hawk employs a combination of Random Forest machine learning algorithms and physical behavior analysis using K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), along with live monitoring feeds for real-time detection and prevention. Additionally, Hawk ensures the secure storage and transfer of data using homomorphic encryption, which allows data to be processed while remaining encrypted, thereby safeguarding passenger information from unauthorized access.

Research Problem

As air travel in Sri Lanka continues to grow, the need for efficient and secure airport operations has never been greater. The current process is struggling to keep up with the increasing passenger volumes, leading to significant challenges that impact both passengers and airport staff

Identified research problems:

  • Manual check-ins and security checks result in lengthy queues and delays, causing frustration for passengers and potential missed flights.
  • The reliance on human intervention for every step of the process leads to bottlenecks, errors, and inconsistencies, reducing overall airport efficiency.
  • The manual verification of passenger identities and documentation is prone to human error and can be exploited for identity fraud and unauthorized access, compromising airport security.
Research Objectives

Main Objective

The main objective of Hawk is to revolutionize the airport departure process in Sri Lanka by automating key procedures to enhance efficiency, reduce wait times, and improve overall passenger satisfaction while simultaneously implementing advanced security measures to protect against emerging threats and ensure the safety and integrity of the system.

Specific Objectives
  • Utilize advanced facial landmark detection technology for accurate passenger identity verification.
  • Ensure quick and reliable biometric authentication to enhance security and streamline the departure process.
  • Employ Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) protocols to authenticate access without revealing sensitive information.
  • Ensure only authorized personnel can access restricted areas and systems, maintaining the integrity and security of airport operations.
  • Integrate Random Forest machine learning algorithms to detect and prevent fraudulent activities.
  • Implement physical behavior analysis using K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) and a live monitoring feed to enhance physical security.
  • Ensure robust protection against various forms of fraud through a multi-layered approach.
  • Ensure secure storage and transfer of sensitive data through homomorphic encryption.
